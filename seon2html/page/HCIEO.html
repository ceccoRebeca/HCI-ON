4. Concepts Definition

The following table shows the definitions for HCIEO concepts.

Concept Definition
HCI Evaluation HCI Evaluation: Intentional Event (Complex Action) caused by the HCI Evaluator Intention. Systematic determination of the extent to which an Human-Computer Interaction meets its HCI Evaluation criteria
________________________
ISO 9241-220: 2019
Evaluation: systematic determination of the extent to which an entity meets its specified criteria

ISO 9241-220: 2019
Inspection-based evaluation: evaluation based on the judgment of one or more evaluator(s) who examine or use a system to identify potential usability problemas and/or deviations from established criteria

Note: inspection-based evaluation is a generic ter for methods that include, but are not limited to, heuristic evaluation, cognitive walkthroughs, standards inspection, pluralistic walkthroughs, and consistency inspections

ISO 25066:2019
Inspection-based evaluation: evaluation based on the judgment of one or more evaluator(s) who examine or use a system to identify potential usability problems (including deviations from established criteria).

Note: The evaluators making the inspections typically are usability specialists but can also include end users and members of the design team.

ISO 9241-220: 2019
User-based evaluation: evaluation that involves representative users performing tasks with the system to enable identification of human-centred quality problems and/or measurements of efficiency, effectiveness, user satisfaction or other user experiences

ISO 25066:2019
User-based evaluation: evaluation that involves representative users performing tasks with the system to enable identification of usability problems and/or measurements of efficiency, effectiveness, user satisfaction or other user experiences

ISO 25066:2019
Usability problem: situation during use resulting in poor effectiveness, efficiency or satisfaction

ISO 25066:2019
Usability defect: product attribute(s) that lead(s) to a mismatch between user intentions and/or user actions and the system attributes and behavior

Note 1: typical usability defects include the following: additional unnecessary steps not required as part of completing a task; misleading information; insufficient and/or poor information on the user interface; unexpected system responses; limitations in navigation; inefficient use error recovery mechanism; physical characteristics of the user interface that are not suitable for the physical characteristics of the user.

Note 2: Deviations of product attributes of the object of evaluation from established criteria are also usability defects.

ISO 9241-220: 2019
Human-centred quality: extent to which requirements for usability, accessibility, user experience and avoidance of harm from user are met.

Note 1: Human-centred quality is a collective term for the intended outcomes of interaction of the user with the system

ISO 25060: 2010
Usability objective: stated level of usability expressed in terms of effectiveness, efficiency and satisfaction in a specified context of use which can be verified

Note: usability objectives can be stated as user requirements, in which case the level to be achieved is a usability requirement, or they can be stated as desired "target levels" depending on their use in design and evaluation

ISO 25060: 2010
Usability inspection: evaluation based on the considered judgment of evaluators who examine the usability-related aspects of an interface with respect to specified criteria

Note: usability inspection is the generic term for several methods, including but not limited to heuristic evaluation, cognitive walkthroughs, standards inspection, pluralistic walkthroughs, and consistency inspections.

ISO 25060: 2010
usability testing: evaluation that involves representative users performing specific tasks with the system to enable the measurement of efficiency, effectiveness, and/or user satisfaction

ISO 25060: 2010
usability-walkthrough: usability evaluation in which one or more evaluators step through a scenario playing the role of a user and identifying usability problems associated with successful completion of the scenario.

ISO/IEC 14598-5:1998
Evaluation method: a procedure describing the action to be performed by the evaluator in order to obtain the result for the specified measurement or verification applied on the specified product components or on the product as a whole

ISO/TR 16982: 2002
usability method: method supporting human-centred design used for the purpose of increasing the usability of a product or a system

ISO/TS 18152:2010
Formative evaluation: evaluation designed and used to improve the object of evaluation, especially when it is still being developed

ISO/TS 18152:2010
Summative evaluation: evaluation designed to present conclusions about the merit or worth of the object of evaluation and recommendations about whether it should be retained, altered or eliminated
HCI Evaluation Artifact
HCI Evaluation Criteria HCI Evaluation Criteria: Evaluation criteria specified according to the HCI Evaluation Goal of a certain HCI Evaluation.
____________
ISO 9241-220:2019
Typically include user requirements, usability guidelines in standards, design conventions contained in manufacturer guidelines and style guides, task models to be supported as well as standardized principles (ISO 9241-220:2019). Could be related to software or hardware evaluation.
The evaluative criteria can include good practice and/or documented principles, guidelines, requirements or standards (ISO/TR 25060:2010).
HCI Evaluation Method HCI Evaluation Methods: a procedure describing the actions to be perfomed by the evaluator in an HCI Evaluation.

______
ISO/IEC 14598-5:1998
Evaluation method: a procedure describing the action to be performed by the evaluator in order to obtain the result for the specified measurement or verification applied on the specified product components or on the product as a whole

ISO/TR 16982: 2002
usability method: method supporting human-centred design used for the purpose of increasing the usability of a product or a system
HCI Evaluation Report HCI Evaluation Report: Document that presents evaluation results and other information relevant to an evaluation
_____________
ISO/IEC 14698-5:1998
Evaluation report: Document that presents evaluation results and other information relevant to an evaluation
The content of evaluation reports can vary based on the objectives of the evaluation (ISO/IEC TR 25060:2010).
HCI Evaluator HCI Evaluator: individual or organization (Stakeholder) that performs an evaluation (ISO/IEC 25040:2011).
______________
Can include usability specialists, developers, end users or other types of professionals (ISO/TR 25060:2010)
HCI Evaluator Goal The propositional content of something the HCI Evaluator wants to achieve (HCI Evaluator Intention) by perfoming an HCI Evaluation. E.g., measure the usability of an User Interface being used by the users.
HCI Evaluator Intention An Intention that inheres in a HCI Evaluator, causing him to perform intentional HCI Evaluation. E.g., the intention to evaluate an interactive computer system.
HCI Quality Characteristic Quality Characteristics: are qualities that inheres in Human-Computer Interaction. Ex.: the UI usability, the user performance.
Quality Characteristics inheres in both interactive computer software and hardware that constitutes the Interactive Computer System. Ex: usability of a interactive software system (software), screen size of a smart watch (hardware), among others.
The Quality Characteristic can be considered for the whole Interactive Computer System or part of it, e.g., its User Interface, Input Equipment.
____________
Usability can be considered at various levels of scope and complexity (from an entire complex system to individual components of a system, product, service or environment) (ISO 9241-11:2018).
For complex products, usability can be considered for the whole product or part of the product (ISO 9241-11:2018).
